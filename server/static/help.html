Welcome to TradeBot
TradeBot is an automated algorithmic trading platform that connects to your brokerage accounts, runs strategy-based signals on a 60-second engine tick, enforces risk limits, and lets you place manual orders — all from one dashboard.
10 Built-in Strategies
Momentum, RSI Mean Reversion, MACD+Volume, Bollinger Bands, Golden Cross, SMA Crossover, 52-Week Breakout, and more.
10-Layer Risk Engine
Kill switch, daily/weekly loss limits, PDT protection, max positions, symbol exposure, blacklist, and trading hours.
Multi-Account Trading
Connect multiple broker accounts and route each strategy independently. Paper and live accounts side by side.
Backtesting Studio
Run any strategy against historical data with slippage, commission, and capital controls. Save and compare runs.
Start in Paper Trading mode. TradeBot defaults to Alpaca's paper trading sandbox. No real money is at risk until you explicitly add a live account and enable a strategy on it.
First-time Setup
Get TradeBot running in under 5 minutes with these steps.
Prerequisites
RequirementDetails
Python 3.11+TradeBot server requires Python 3.11 or higher. Check with python --version.
Alpaca AccountFree at alpaca.markets. A paper trading account is created automatically with no funding needed.
Alpaca API KeysFrom your Alpaca dashboard under API Keys. You need both the API key ID and the secret key.
Installation & Launch
1
Install dependencies
In the TradeBot directory, run: pip install -r requirements.txt
2
Create your .env file
Copy .env.example to .env and fill in your Alpaca API credentials. Set ALPACA_ACCOUNT_TYPE=paper to start safely.
3
Start the server
Run: uvicorn server.main:app --reload --port 8000
Then open http://localhost:8000 in your browser.
4
Complete the setup wizard
On first launch you'll see the Setup page. Set your password, starting capital, daily loss limit, and pick your first strategy. This takes about 60 seconds.
5
Explore the dashboard
You'll land on the Dashboard showing your account equity, engine status, recent signals, and allocation. The engine ticks every 60 seconds during market hours.
Keep your .env file private. It contains your API credentials. Never commit it to version control or share it.
Broker Accounts
TradeBot supports multiple broker accounts simultaneously. Each account has its own credentials, paper/live status, and strategy assignments.
Adding an Account
1
Navigate to Broker Accounts
Click Broker Accounts in the left sidebar.
2
Click "Add Account"
Fill in a label (e.g., "Alpaca Paper"), your API key, secret key, and whether it's paper or live.
3
Verify the connection
Click Test Connection — TradeBot will attempt to fetch your account balance to confirm credentials are valid.
4
Assign strategies
Go to Bots & Strategies and enable strategies for this account. Each strategy × account pair can be independently toggled.
Switching Accounts

On the Positions & Orders and Balances pages, an account dropdown appears in the top header. Click it to switch which account's data is displayed. The order ticket also has an account selector so manual orders go to the right account.

Credential Security

All API keys and secrets are encrypted with AES-256 (Fernet) before being stored in the database. The encryption key is stored in your .env file as DB_SECRET_KEY — generated automatically on first setup.

If you lose your DB_SECRET_KEY, stored broker credentials cannot be decrypted. Back up your .env file securely.
Strategies & Bots
TradeBot comes with 9 automated strategies. Each one generates buy/sell signals that the engine evaluates every 60 seconds during market hours.
Strategy Reference
StrategyHow it worksBest for
Momentum Scans top actives/gainers. Enters when price is above its 20-day SMA and RSI is between 40–70. Trending markets, high-volume days
RSI Mean Reversion Buys when RSI drops below 30 (oversold). Exits when RSI crosses 50. Range-bound stocks, post-selloff rebounds
MACD + Volume Enters on bullish MACD crossover confirmed by above-average volume. Momentum with volume confirmation
Bollinger Bands Buys when price touches lower band; exits at middle band. Reversal strategy. Mean-reverting, low-volatility stocks
Golden Cross Enters when the 50-day SMA crosses above the 200-day SMA. Long-term trend following
SMA Crossover Fast (10-day) SMA crosses above slow (30-day) SMA. Short-term trend detection
52-Week Breakout Buys when price closes above its 52-week high on strong volume. Breakout trading, momentum stocks
Enabling a Strategy
1
Open Bots & Strategies
Click it in the left sidebar. You'll see two panels — accounts on the left, strategies on the right.
2
Select an account
Click a broker account in the left panel to see which strategies are assigned to it.
3
Toggle strategies on/off
Use the toggle next to each strategy to enable it for that account. Disabled strategies are skipped by the engine.
4
Adjust parameters
Expand a strategy to edit its parameters — notional size, RSI thresholds, SMA periods, etc. Changes take effect on the next engine tick.
The engine only runs strategies during US market hours (9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET). You can override this with Run Engine Now in the Dashboard for testing.
Position Sizing

Each strategy has a notional parameter — the dollar amount per trade (e.g., $500 means each signal buys $500 worth of stock). You can also set position sizing to a % of portfolio in Risk settings, which overrides the per-strategy notional.

Manual Orders
Place trades directly from the Positions & Orders page without waiting for a strategy signal.
Using the Order Ticket
1
Select an account
Use the account dropdown inside the order ticket to pick which broker account to trade on.
2
Choose Buy or Sell
Click the Buy (green) or Sell (red) tab at the top of the ticket.
3
Enter a symbol and get a quote
Type a ticker symbol (e.g., AAPL) and click Quote to see the live bid/ask mid-price.
4
Set quantity or dollar amount
Switch between Shares and Dollars using the segmented control. Enter your desired quantity. An estimated cost appears below.
5
Place the order
Click Place Buy Order / Place Sell Order. A market order is submitted instantly. Results appear below the button with the order ID and status.
Manual orders bypass the strategy engine and no-trade list. They execute immediately as market orders. Only use this when you know what you're doing.

Risk Controls
TradeBot enforces 10 configurable risk guards on every strategy signal before an order is placed. These run in order — the first failing guard blocks the trade.
Guard Reference
GuardWhat it does
Kill SwitchMaster emergency stop. When on, ALL automated trading halts immediately. Manual orders still work.
Daily Loss LimitHalts trading when today's P&L drops below N% of starting equity.
Weekly Loss LimitSame as daily but measured over the rolling 7-day window.
Consecutive LossesBlocks new trades after N losses in a row. Resets when you manually reset or a trade wins.
PDT ProtectionPrevents a 4th day-trade in a 5-day rolling window on accounts under $25,000.
Max PositionsCaps total open positions. New entries are blocked until an existing position is closed.
Symbol ExposurePrevents allocating more than N% of portfolio to a single symbol.
Max Orders/DayLimits total orders submitted in one trading day.
Trading HoursOnly allows orders between the start and end times you configure (ET).
No-Trade ListSymbols on the blacklist are never traded, regardless of signals.
Configuring Risk Settings
1
Open Risk in the sidebar
The Risk page shows your current P&L, engine status, and all configurable guards in one place.
2
Edit a value
Click the numeric input next to any guard and type a new value, then click Save next to it. Changes apply immediately on the next engine tick.
3
Add symbols to the no-trade list
Type a ticker in the No-Trade List field and press Enter or click Add. Chips appear for each blocked symbol. Click the × on a chip to remove it.
Recommended starting settings: Max daily loss 2%, max positions 5, trading hours 9:30–15:45 ET, PDT protection on. These prevent the most common beginner mistakes.
Kill Switch
The kill switch is your emergency stop. One click halts all automated strategy trading instantly.
When to use it

Activate the kill switch when: the market is behaving unexpectedly, you see a strategy generating bad signals, you're leaving for the day and don't want unsupervised trading, or you've hit an uncomfortable loss level.

How to activate

The kill switch card is at the top of the Risk page. Click the red Activate Kill Switch button. The banner turns red across all pages. Click Deactivate to resume.

The kill switch does not close existing positions. It only stops new orders from being placed. To close all positions, use the close-all button on the Positions page.

Interpreting the fill rate

Fill rate = filled signals ÷ total signals. A low fill rate (below 50%) means risk guards are blocking most of your signals. Check the Risk page and Logs to understand which guard is triggering most often.

Backtesting
Test any strategy against historical price data before risking real money. Saved runs persist for future comparison.
Running a backtest
1
Choose a strategy
Select from the strategy dropdown. All 9 strategies are available for backtesting.
2
Enter symbols
Type one or more ticker symbols separated by commas (e.g., AAPL, MSFT, NVDA).
3
Set the date range and capital
Pick start/end dates (minimum 30 days recommended), initial capital, position size %, commission %, and slippage %.
4
Run and review
Click Run Backtest. Results show total return, win rate, max drawdown, Sharpe ratio, and a full trade log. Name the run to save it.
Key metrics explained
MetricMeaning
Total ReturnNet profit as a % of starting capital over the test period.
Win Rate% of trades that closed with a profit.
Max DrawdownLargest peak-to-trough decline. Lower is better. Over 20% is a warning sign.
Sharpe RatioRisk-adjusted return. Above 1.0 is acceptable; above 2.0 is excellent.
Avg Trade P&LAverage dollar profit/loss per completed trade.
Past performance does not predict future results. Backtests use daily bar data and assume limit-less liquidity. Real execution has wider spreads and slippage, especially on low-volume stocks.

Filtering logs

Use the filter tabs at the top of the table to show All, Filled, Blocked, or Error signals. This helps you quickly diagnose whether a strategy is producing signals but being blocked (risk issue) vs. producing no signals at all (strategy logic or market condition issue).

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions and their answers.
Why is my strategy not placing any orders?

Check these in order: (1) Is the strategy enabled for a broker account on the Bots page? (2) Is a risk guard blocking it? Check the Logs page for "blocked" signals. (3) Is the market open? The engine only runs during US trading hours. (4) Does the symbol meet the strategy's criteria? Use backtesting to verify the strategy generates signals on your symbols.

What is PDT and how does it affect me?

The Pattern Day Trader (PDT) rule applies to US accounts under $25,000. It limits you to 3 day-trades (buying and selling the same stock in a single day) within any rolling 5-business-day period. TradeBot's PDT guard automatically blocks a 4th day-trade to protect your account. If you're over $25,000, you can disable the PDT guard in Risk settings.

Can I run TradeBot 24/7?

Yes — the server runs continuously. The engine only evaluates strategies during configured trading hours (default 9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET). Outside of hours, the engine ticks but skips order placement.

How do I add a symbol that the scanner misses?

Each strategy has a symbols parameter you can override. Expand the strategy on the Bots page and add custom symbols to its symbol list. The strategy will then always evaluate those symbols regardless of scanner output.

Where is my data stored?

All data is stored locally in trading.db (SQLite). No data is sent to external servers except to your configured broker API. Backtest history, signals, settings, and encrypted credentials all live in this single file.

How do I reset consecutive losses?

Go to the Risk page and click the Reset button next to the Consecutive Losses field. This manually clears the counter so strategies can trade again.

Is my API key safe?

Yes — keys are encrypted with AES-256 before storage. The secret key is never returned by the API (only the last 4 characters of the public key are shown for identification). That said, use Alpaca's Trading only scope when generating keys — not the full "transfer funds" scope.

Still stuck? Check the server log (server.log in the TradeBot directory) for detailed error messages from the engine and API calls.
